Amsterdam Attractions

Keukenhof Gardens

Recognised as "Gardens of the World," the Keukenhof Gardens are a must-visit attraction in Amsterdam. This largest flower garden in the world displays millions of flowers, including tulips, daffodils, and hyacinths, turning the garden into a kaleidoscope of colors. The garden is open from mid-March to mid-May, and visitors can take a leisurely stroll among the flowers, enjoy the tranquility of the garden, or explore the variety of plants and flowers on display. Don't forget to ride the miniature train, the old and charming bus, or the fun water taxi to get a new perspective on this magnificent attraction.

Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum is a national museum of the Netherlands located in Amsterdam, and it is one of the most famous and largest art museums in the world. This museum promises to take you on an epic journey through the Netherlands' fascinating history, including its cultural and artistic achievements. Visit the Rijksmuseum and explore its stunning collection of over 800 year-round exhibitions from the Netherlands and Europe. Discover and experience the Dutch Golden Age, famous artists such as Rembrandt and Vermeer, and the rich and diverse history of this fascinating country.

Vondelpark

Vondelpark is a popular urban park in Amsterdam's Oud-Zuid borough. Built on the initiative of poet and playwright Joost van Vondel, Vondelpark is a masterpiece of landscape architecture demonstrating some French and English influences. Expanding over 45 hectares of beautiful greenery and featuring stunning lakefront views, Vondelpark serves as a beloved and peaceful retreat for locals and visitors alike. This beautiful park is home to many families of swans, rabbits and lovely gardens among other wildlife. End a wonderful day exploring the beauty of Amsterdam with a leisurely boat ride on the Vondelpark Lake.
